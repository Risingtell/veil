Stellar Hacks: Real-World ZK

Private payouts that prove
their own compliance

Veil is a privacy pool on Stellar where every withdrawal proves, in zero-knowledge, that the funds came from a real deposit inside a compliance-approved set — privacy for honest users, exclusion of bad actors, in a single proof.

The problem

Privacy without compliance is a dead end

A plain mixer is indistinguishable from money laundering, so banks, payroll providers and aid organizations can't touch it. Veil fixes the trade-off the way the Privacy Pools paper proposes — provable compliance baked into every private withdrawal.

Plain mixers

  • Anyone can hide, including bad actors
  • No way to prove funds are clean
  • Regulators are locked out entirely
  • Institutions won't use them

Veil

  • Withdrawals prove membership in an approved set
  • Zero-knowledge — no amounts or links revealed
  • A regulator view-key for full auditability
  • Built for payroll & aid disbursement
How it works

Zero-knowledge that is load-bearing

The Soroban contract releases nothing unless a real Groth16 proof verifies on-chain via Stellar's new Protocol 26 BN254 pairing_check host function. Remove the ZK and the product doesn't exist.

🔒

Verified on-chain

A single Circom circuit proves note ownership, deposit-tree membership, approved-set membership, and an unspent nullifier — checked by the contract itself, not a server.

💸

Private payout

Deposit a fixed denomination; later withdraw to a fresh, unlinkable address by submitting a proof. Live on testnet: a real 1 XLM private payout with on-chain double-spend protection.

🛡️

Compliance gate

Every withdrawal must also prove the note is in an Association Set Provider's approved set. Honest users stay private; flagged deposits are provably un-withdrawable.

The differentiator

Auditor view-key — private and auditable

A designated regulator holds a BabyJubJub view-key. At deposit time, an encrypted record is published on-chain, so the regulator's ability to investigate never depends on a depositor keeping data.

🌐 The public sees

  • Only ciphertext. Withdrawals are unlinkable — nobody can connect a payout to a deposit or a person.

🔑 The auditor sees

  • Decrypts any record with the view-key, then hashes the nullifier to trace an anonymous withdrawal back to a real identity — on demand.
